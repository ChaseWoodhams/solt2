FAQ

Can I trial Webflow before paying?

keyboard_arrow_down

Sure! You can test out Webflow on our free plan where you can experiment with 2 projects. Your unhosted projects will have a two-page limit, but you can purchase a site plan on a per-project basis to unlock up to 100 static pages and additional CMS pages.

What is a project?

keyboard_arrow_down

A project is a website that you build in Webflow. You can publish projects to a webflow.io staging subdomain for free, export the code on a paid plan, or add a site plan to connect your custom domain and unlock hosting features.

What can I white label?

keyboard_arrow_down

Pro accounts can add their own logo to Client Billing forms and the Editor. Pro accounts can also remove references to Webflow in the source code and form submission emails, and hide the Webflow badge from their staging sites.

How much traffic can the hosting handle?

keyboard_arrow_down

Webflow hosting scales automatically to handle millions of concurrent visits. All site plans serve sites through our Amazon's Cloudfront CDN and accelerated using Fastly, loading sites in milliseconds.

What kind of support does Webflow provide?

keyboard_arrow_down

We offer fast email support to paid accounts and prioritized help for team accounts. Community support (forum.webflow.com) is available to free accounts.

How long does it take to learn Webflow?

keyboard_arrow_down

If you're new to building websites, our video tutorials will get up and running quickly. If you already know concepts behind CSS and the box model, you will feel at home in Webflow.

FAQ

These are some questions my patients often ask

Are these services covered by insurance?

v

While insurance can be helpful in many circumstances, such as life and death hospitalizations or yearly physical screening exams, when it comes to truly healing the body and the desire to overcome chronic conditions, the insurance model does not give us the flexibility to truly customize the healing experience that you need. If it did, you wouldn't be here :)

What is your success rate?

v

No guarantees of general outcomes can be made, but Dr. Solt does boast a 90% success rate. This is determined by whether or not each patient reaches their specific goals within a 6 month time frame. The high percentage is also due to the fact that Dr. Solt will only work with patients that she is confident she can help.

Who is not a candidate for treatment?

v

Generally, people with active cancer, people who are smokers, and people who have received a steroid injection or other steroid treatment within the last 6 weeks are not candidates for treatment. However, Dr. Solt will sit down with you and go through your history in detail to determine whether or not you qualify.

How much do these procedures cost?

v

Specific treatment plans are created for each patient, depending on their history, and many other factors that Dr. Solt takes into consideration. Prices can range anywhere from $500 - $8000, and she is always mindful to work with you and your budget to get you the care you need.

I have a lot of health issues going on - does Dr. Solt provide long term care?

v

Dr. Solt is not a primary care physician, but she does offer long term healing programs (3 months, 6 months, or 12 months) that are meant to address hormone imbalances, get you entirely out of pain, reverse your biological age, and thoroughly ensure every cell in your body has optimal nutrient levels. Extensive blood testing is done and Dr. Solt spends hours with you to curate custom healing. These packages also include access to Dr. Solt via text message whenever you have a question, an in-home pantry audit, same-day appointments Mon-Fri (pending availability), and more! They start at $2500/month after a $750 on-boarding fee. Due to the extremely detailed personal care and high level concierge service in these packages, she only takes a handful of patients at once and does not accept everyone. Please contact the office for more detail.

Does Dr. Solt work with children?

v

Dr. Solt loves children, and has a toddler of her own, but unfortunately she does not see anyone under 18 in her practice.
